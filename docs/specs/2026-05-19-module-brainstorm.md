# Module Brainstorm — 2026-05-19

Source: ongoing kids' business model learning module. Current portal has
6 lessons live (Garage Sale, Chick-fil-A, Lemonade Stand, Pet Shop,
YouTube Creator, Cookie Bakery). Goal of this doc: spec out the *next*
generation of modules with strong real-world practice loops.

## Brainstorm philosophy

Every module should pass three tests:

1. **Teaches a distinct concept** the existing 6 don't already cover.
2. **Has a kid-runnable real-world mission** (no money required for the
   parent to greenlight; safe; doable in 1 week or less).
3. **Maps to a recognizable real business** so kids see the concept
   playing out in the adult world.

If a module can't pass #2, it's a textbook, not a practice loop.

## Tier 1 — Core Economic Concepts (highest priority)

Modules that introduce a *new* fundamental idea kids haven't met yet.

### 1.1 🏦 Piggy Bank Power-Up
- **Concept**: Saving + compound interest ("money that makes money")
- **Why it matters**: The single most important number-sense lesson.
- **Real-world**: Save a fixed % of allowance for 4 weeks; chart growth.
- **Twist**: Parent matches 10% weekly → kid sees the compounding effect.
- **Card**: Savings Sensei (Uncommon)
- **Famous example**: Warren Buffett (started at age 11 with $114.75)

### 1.2 📈 Stock Market Adventure
- **Concept**: Buying ownership of a company; share price moves with
  perception of the business.
- **Real-world**: Pick 3 imaginary stocks of brands the kid knows
  (Disney, Lego, McDonald's). Look up close price daily for 10 days,
  sketch the line. Reflect: "What news might have moved the price?"
- **Tools**: Yahoo Finance for closing prices (no account needed).
- **Card**: Stock Trader (Rare — already exists, just hook it up)

### 1.3 ❤️ Charity Lab
- **Concept**: Doing good as a real activity, not a tax write-off.
  Setting a goal, recruiting helpers, tracking progress.
- **Real-world**: Pick a cause that matters to the kid (animal shelter,
  food bank, library, refugee aid). Set a $ goal. Run a mini-fundraiser
  (bake sale + donation jar, chore-a-thon, donation drive).
- **Card**: Heart of Gold (Rare)

### 1.4 🛡️ Risk Pool Island (Insurance Lite)
- **Concept**: Many small contributions can absorb one big loss.
- **Real-world**: Run an "umbrella" with siblings/cousins — each pays
  10¢/day. When a toy breaks or someone misses out on a treat, the
  pool covers up to $X. Track who pays in, who pays out.
- **Card**: Risk Master (Rare)

### 1.5 🏛️ Tax Town
- **Concept**: Where tax money comes from and where it goes
  (schools/roads/parks/safety).
- **Real-world**: Allocate a "family tax" out of the kid's weekly
  earnings (5%) that goes into a shared family fund used for chosen
  shared expenses (game night, library trip). Kid sees the system in
  miniature.
- **Card**: Tax Whiz (Uncommon — already exists)

### 1.6 🔁 Subscription Sense
- **Concept**: Recurring revenue + churn. Why Netflix is worth more
  than a one-time movie sale.
- **Real-world**: Sign up family members for a "Helper Subscription"
  ($1/month → you make their bed once a week). Track who renews vs.
  cancels and why.
- **Card**: Recurring Royal (Rare)

## Tier 2 — Real Famous Business Deep-Dives

Modules anchored to a brand kids already know.

### 2.1 🍔 McDonald's: The Real Estate Empire
- **Hidden truth**: McD's makes more $ from owning land + leasing to
  franchisees than from burgers. Real estate is the moat.
- **Real-world**: Spot 3 fast-food locations and notice WHY they're
  located there (highway exits, busy corners, school adjacency).
- **Card**: Location Genius (Uncommon)

### 2.2 🎮 Roblox: The Platform Economy
- **Concept**: Roblox makes money when *others* make money on it. The
  platform = a flat venue, the games = the products.
- **Real-world**: Design a paper / cardboard game; "sell" plays to
  siblings for tokens. Track plays + earnings.
- **Card**: Game Maker (Rare)

### 2.3 🛍️ Costco: Why You Pay to Be a Customer
- **Concept**: The membership fee IS the profit. Bulk pricing +
  loss-leader rotisserie chickens to pull people in.
- **Real-world**: With a parent, visit any membership store; find 3
  items where bulk pricing saves >20%. Sketch the breakeven point.
- **Card**: Member Master (Uncommon)

### 2.4 🚚 Amazon: How Did It Get Here So Fast?
- **Concept**: Logistics + warehouse network + "the day one mindset."
- **Real-world**: Track a real Amazon package using the order page;
  every status update goes on a tracker (Ordered → Shipped → Out for
  delivery → Delivered). Map the journey.
- **Card**: Logistics Lord (Rare)

### 2.5 🎨 Lego: Bricks Forever
- **Concept**: Compatibility-as-moat (1958 brick still fits 2026
  brick). Selling to kids AND adults.
- **Real-world**: Build something new from your existing bricks (no
  buying). Show it to family, get bids on what they'd pay for it.
- **Card**: Brick Builder (Rare)

### 2.6 🐭 Disney Magic: Characters That Earn for 100 Years
- **Concept**: Intellectual property compounds. Mickey (1928) still
  prints money.
- **Real-world**: Create your own original character + give them one
  short story. Try to "license" your character (with parents'
  permission) to a sibling's project.
- **Card**: IP Inventor (Legendary)

## Tier 3 — Practical Hustle Modules

Each one is a small business a kid could actually run.

### 3.1 🌽 Farm Stand
- **Concept**: Production + seasonality. Things grow in cycles.
- **Real-world**: Grow herbs/microgreens/sprouts on a windowsill (1-3
  weeks). Sell to family for cooking. Track grow time vs. price.
- **Card**: Farm Hand (Uncommon)

### 3.2 ❄️ Snow Day Hustle (or Sun Day Hustle)
- **Concept**: Seasonal services. Match supply to demand windows.
- **Real-world**: Shovel a neighbor's walk, rake leaves, wash a car —
  whatever the season is. Earn $20 in one weekend, track time spent
  vs. dollars earned (your hourly rate).
- **Card**: Hustle Hero (Uncommon)

### 3.3 🤝 Deal Maker
- **Concept**: Negotiation as a fair value exchange, not a fight.
  BATNA, win-win, the art of listening first.
- **Real-world**: Negotiate one real thing with a parent — allowance
  raise, screen-time extension, dessert deal. Write what you offered
  and what you got.
- **Card**: Negotiator (Rare)

### 3.4 📣 Marketing Mountain
- **Concept**: AIDA model (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action),
  word of mouth, jingles.
- **Real-world**: Pick a real product at home (or a chore service) and
  design a poster + 3-line pitch. Test pitch on 3 family members.
- **Card**: Marketing Maven (Uncommon)

### 3.5 💡 Inventor's Lab
- **Concept**: Ideas can be valuable. Design thinking → prototype →
  feedback → v2.
- **Real-world**: Find a small annoyance at home (cables tangling,
  shoes piling, etc.). Design a fix. Build a paper / cardboard
  prototype. Get 3 family members to try it.
- **Card**: Inventor (Rare)

### 3.6 🧠 Survey Master
- **Concept**: Ask before you build. Why customer research saves money.
- **Real-world**: Pick a product idea (a new flavor of cookie, a video
  channel topic, etc.). Run a 10-person survey with 3 questions. Make
  a decision based on the data.
- **Card**: Research Pro (Uncommon)

## Cross-cutting upgrades to do alongside

These aren't new modules; they multiply the value of all 12+ once
in place.

- **Server sync for missions** — mirror `api/cards.ts` (one endpoint,
  one hook update). Right now missions only live in localStorage.
- **Welcome-back follow-up** — on lesson re-entry when a mission is
  in `accepted`, Momo opens with "Did you try X? Tell me how it
  went!" → links straight to reflection form.
- **Add missions to Chick-fil-A + Garage Sale** — currently lessons
  without practice loops. Five minutes each:
  - Chick-fil-A: "Say 'my pleasure' for 3 days; count how many times.
    Notice how people react."
  - Garage Sale: "Pick 5 toys you don't use → list, price, sell or
    donate. Track what each one earned vs. your guess."
- **Skill ladder on /cards** — group cards by the skill they represent
  (Pricing / Service / Branding / Operations / Saving / Investing),
  not just by rarity. Visual progress toward "mastery" in each lane.
